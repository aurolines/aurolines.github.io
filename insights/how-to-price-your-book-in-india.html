How print cost is calculated

KDP and Pothi both use page count, ink type (BW/color), trim size and paper quality.

Short formula to remember:

Printing cost ≈ Base cost (setup) + per-page cost × page count + color surcharge (if any)

KDP exposes a calculator (but their India delivery and pricing can be confusing). Pothi gives an India-friendly print cost and shipping options — use both to compare final buyer price.

Simple pricing formula (net revenue focus)

Set an MRP that covers printing + platform fee + your target margin.

Use this to calculate the minimum MRP to keep you profitable:

Printing_Cost + Platform_Fee + GST(if applicable) + Desired_Margin = Minimum_MRP

Example (approx):

  • Printing cost (KDP India) = ₹120
  • Platform/royalty take (KDP) = 40% of list price (for paperbacks; KDP has its own royalty rules)
  • Desired margin (you) = ₹150

Work backward to find a viable list price that covers these.

Pricing psychology & common strategies

How customers perceive price — and what price bands sell better.

  • Psychological thresholds: ₹199, ₹249, ₹299, ₹349 — many buyers anchor to these familiar price points.
  • Perceived value: hardcover and thicker paper => higher perceived quality → justify higher price.
  • Regional pricing: consider lower price on India-specific print (Pothi) vs global KDP pricing.

Competitor & market check (fast method)

Quick steps to find the correct price band for your genre and length.

  1. Search 5 books in your genre on Amazon & Pothi with similar page counts.
  2. Note the MRPs and delivery availability in India.
  3. Pick the median price band — adjust ± one band based on quality (paper, cover type).

Practical checklist — set your final price

A short checklist you can use before publishing.

  1. Use KDP and Pothi calculators for exact printing costs for your chosen trim & paper.
  2. Decide target margin → calculate Minimum_MRP.
  3. Run competitor check — choose a nearby psychological price band.
  4. Set India-specific POD price on Pothi; set global price on KDP (you can offer region-specific pricing).

Frequently asked (SEO-friendly)

Should I price paperback lower than hardcover?
Yes — hardcovers typically have higher print costs and carry premium pricing; paperbacks are usually priced lower to capture more buyers.
Does KDP charge GST on printing in India?
KDP's invoices and tax treatment depend on the marketplace and print location — always check the pricing breakdown and consult current KDP docs when in doubt.
Can I set different prices for India and global markets?
Yes — KDP allows regional pricing; Pothi is India-specific so you can set a local price there.
