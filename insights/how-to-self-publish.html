1 — Quick checklist — publish-ready (expand)
Pre-launch checklist (zero-cost options included)
  • Final manuscript (proofread — peer swap reviews keep cost 0)
  • Cover: design on Canva (free) using KDP templates
  • ISBN: optional — KDP provides ASIN / free identifiers for print if acceptable
  • Formatted EPUB (Calibre/Free tools) + print-ready PDF
  • Book description, keywords, categories — optimise for Amazon & search engines
  • Author profile & links (free on Amazon Author Central, Pothi, Goodreads)

Note: publish with **0 ₹ outlay** using free tools and print-on-demand (Pothi / KDP) — printing cost is covered by buyer in POD mode.

2 — Formatting & file preparation (expand)

Use free tools: Canva for cover + KDP templates, Calibre or Word templates for EPUB/PDF. Validate EPUB in Kindle Previewer. For print PDFs ensure margins and bleed match KDP/Pothi templates.

  • 2.0–2.5 line-height recommended for print readability
  • Export images at 300 DPI for print covers (Canva free supports this)
3 — Costs & distribution (India — updated)

Reality: you can self-publish with zero upfront cost by using print-on-demand and free author tools.

  • Amazon KDP — free to upload. KDP's print distribution may not always deliver optimally within India for some print options.
  • Pothi.com — recommended for India-focused POD and local delivery. Pothi supports print-when-ordered (no inventory cost). You only incur printing cost when a customer buys the book.

Recommendation: upload ebook to KDP for global reach and set up Pothi for Indian print deliveries (zero stock cost). Use both to maximize reach and delivery reliability.

4 — Publishing flow (exact steps I followed)
  1. Prepare manuscript → choose ebook & print formats
  2. Create cover in Canva (use KDP/Pothi dimensions)
  3. Set up KDP & Pothi accounts → fill metadata & keywords deliberately
  4. Upload files, preview proofs, publish
  5. Claim Amazon Author page & list Pothi product link for Indian readers
5 — Launch marketing — low-cost tactics
  • Use your author website as the hub — publish SEO articles that link to product pages
  • Share short BTS & reels on Instagram and LinkedIn
  • Use micro-influencers and book bloggers for reviews (often trade-copy)
  • Create an email signup and offer a free chapter PDF (hosts such as SlideShare/Scribd double as backlinks)
6 — Backlink & outreach plan (30-day starter)
  1. Week 1: Publish 2 related articles & share on LinkedIn & Medium (canonical to this page)
  2. Week 2: Upload a PDF guide to SlideShare & Scribd; link back to this article
  3. Week 3: Guest post on 1–2 book blogs and add author profile links on Goodreads/Pothi
  4. Week 4: Seed Quora/Reddit answers linking to specific sections (value-first answers)
7 — Results to track (expand)
